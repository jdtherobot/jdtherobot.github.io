FIELD NOTE 01 / THROUGH-LINE

One continuous problem

Reliability begins before the implementation.

Operating technical systems in consequential environments taught me to see reliability as a whole-system property. Architecture matters, but so do constraints, handoffs, incomplete information, and the ability to coordinate work across organizational boundaries.

Independent builds pull that systems view down into code, firmware, hardware, and signal paths. My developing research direction extends it further: learning algorithms, runtimes, hardware, and feedback loops should be evaluated as parts of one operating system—not isolated layers.

FIELD NOTE 03 / DIRECTION

Developing research direction

Adaptive learning is the bridge, not the destination.

Modern machine-learning architectures are highly capable. My interest is in the limitations that become decisive when a system must keep adapting in real time: continual learning, catastrophic forgetting, memory, resource efficiency, embodiment, and closed-loop operation.

Questions guiding my preparation

These are questions I intend to study—not claims of completed research.

  1. Q1

    How can a system adapt continually without erasing what it already knows?

  2. Q2

    How should learning, memory, runtime, and hardware be co-designed under limited resources?

  3. Q3

    Can adaptive methods improve neural-signal decoding and other real-time closed-loop systems?
